# StackSave AI - Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

This document outlines a realistic go-to-market strategy for acquiring the first 1,000 users for StackSave AI. The strategy focuses on low-cost, high-leverage channels appropriate for a niche developer tool.

---

### Target Audience

-   **Primary:** Engineering Managers, Tech Leads, and VPs of Engineering at startups and mid-sized tech companies (10-100 employees).
-   **Secondary:** CTOs and founders at early-stage startups who are budget-conscious.
-   **Tertiary:** Individual developers and small agencies who are curious about their own spending.

### Phase 1: Launch & Initial Traction (First 100 Users)

**Goal:** Validate the tool's usefulness and gather initial feedback.

1.  **Personal Network:**
    -   Share the tool with friends, colleagues, and former coworkers in the tech industry.
    -   Ask for honest feedback on the UI, the audit results, and the overall concept.

2.  **Niche Online Communities:**
    -   **Hacker News:** Prepare a "Show HN" post. The title is crucial: "Show HN: I built a free tool to audit your team's AI spending." The key is to be authentic and engage with every comment.
    -   **Reddit:** Post in relevant subreddits like `r/programming`, `r/webdev`, and `r/softwaregore` (with a title like "I was paying how much for ChatGPT?"). The post should be a story, not a direct ad.
    -   **Indie Hackers:** Share the journey of building the tool. The community is very supportive of solo-developer projects.

3.  **Content Marketing (Seed Content):**
    -   Write one high-quality blog post: "The 5 Most Common Ways Developers Waste Money on AI Tools."
    -   The blog post will have the StackSave AI tool embedded at the end as a free solution.
    -   Share this post on social media and in newsletters.

### Phase 2: Growth & Scaling (100 - 1,000 Users)

**Goal:** Build a repeatable channel for user acquisition and increase brand awareness.

1.  **Product-Led Growth (The Viral Loop):**
    -   The core of the strategy is the **shareable report**. When a user gets a great result (e.g., "$5,000 annual savings"), they are incentivized to share it with their team or boss.
    -   **Action:** Make the report page visually appealing and easy to share. Add "Share on Twitter" and "Copy Link" buttons. Ensure the Open Graph image for the shared link is beautiful and shows the key savings number.

2.  **SEO (Long-term play):**
    -   Create programmatic SEO pages for tool comparisons, e.g., "GitHub Copilot vs. Cursor" or "ChatGPT Team vs. Plus."
    -   These pages will provide genuine value and have a call-to-action to use the audit tool.
    -   Target long-tail keywords like "is chatgpt team worth it" or "github copilot business pricing."

3.  **Social Media:**
    -   **Twitter/X:** Share anonymized, interesting findings from the audits. For example: "🤯 Just saw a 10-person team that could save $8,000/year on their AI stack. The culprit? Enterprise plans for a small team." This creates curiosity and drives traffic.
    -   **LinkedIn:** Post more professional case studies and target the "Engineering Manager" demographic directly.

### Monetization (Future Consideration)

While the core tool will remain free to drive user acquisition, potential monetization strategies include:

-   **"Pro" Consultation:** The existing CTA for users with >$500 savings can be monetized. Offer a paid 1-hour consultation to walk them through a detailed savings plan.
-   **B2B Dashboard:** A paid product for larger companies that automatically and continuously monitors their AI spending by integrating with their billing systems.
-   **Affiliate Links:** For tool recommendations, use affiliate links where appropriate. This must be done transparently to maintain trust.
